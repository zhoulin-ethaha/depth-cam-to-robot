Tune until the raked grooves stand out as clean white centrelines — the right panel updates live, even before you capture. The left panel toggles Depth / RGB. Detection

Valley finds anything a few mm deeper than the surrounding sand — the hand-drawn grooves. Ridge finds raised lines instead. Band keeps a fixed depth interval (use the Band sliders below).

How much deeper than the surrounding surface a pixel must be to count as a groove. Lower it to catch fainter rake marks; raise it if noise/grain gets picked up.

Blur radius used to estimate the smooth bare-sand surface that gets subtracted off. Should be larger than your groove width but smaller than the slow sag/tilt of the sandbox. Larger = flattens broader undulations.

Smooths per-pixel depth noise before detection. Raise it if the grooves come out speckly; lower it to keep the thinnest marks.

Discards detected specks smaller than this many pixels before thinning — clears isolated noise that isn't part of a real groove.

Reject natural grooves

Tools to suppress pre-existing/natural sand grooves and keep only the deliberate hand-raked ones. Each is independent — set any slider to 0 to disable it (so you can compare the difference it makes).

Background subtraction. Capture the undrawn sand with Set Reference, then raise this to subtract that baseline — pre-existing natural grooves appear in both frames and cancel, leaving only what you drew. 0 = off, 1 = full. (Camera and sandbox must stay still between reference and capture.)

Drops whole grooves whose average depth is shallower than this. Hand-raked grooves are consistently a few mm deep; faint natural ripples that only graze the threshold get rejected. 0 = off.

Keep only grooves whose width matches the raking tool. A hand rake makes a consistent width; thin natural scratches or broad dishes fall outside the band. 0 = off (max 0 = no upper limit). Needs a workspace for the mm scale.

Drops grooves shorter than this along their centreline — natural texture tends to break into short fragments. 0 = off. Needs a workspace for the mm scale.

Band mode

Only used in Band mode: keep pixels whose depth (relative to the estimated surface) falls within ±width of the center.

Depth view range

Colormap range for the depth view only (does not affect detection). Leave both at 0 for auto-range.
